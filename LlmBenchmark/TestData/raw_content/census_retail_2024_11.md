# Advance Monthly Sales for Retail and Food Services - November 2024

## U.S. Census Bureau

Advance estimates of U.S. retail and food services sales for November 2024, adjusted for seasonal variation and holiday and trading-day differences, but not for price changes, were $724.6 billion, an increase of 0.7 percent from the previous month, and up 3.8 percent from November 2023. Total sales for the September 2024 through November 2024 period were up 2.9 percent from the same period a year ago.

### Sales by Category

Retail trade sales were up 0.9 percent from October 2024, and up 3.4 percent above last year. Nonstore retailers were up 10.2 percent from November 2023, while food services and drinking places were up 6.0 percent from last year.

Motor vehicle and parts dealers sales were $130.8 billion in November, up 2.6 percent from October. This category represents the largest component of retail sales.

Excluding motor vehicles and parts, retail sales were $589.3 billion, an increase of 0.2 percent from October 2024.

Excluding motor vehicles, parts, and gasoline stations, the so-called "control group" used in GDP calculations, retail sales rose 0.4 percent in November to $512.7 billion.

Electronics and appliance stores reported sales of $9.2 billion, up 1.1 percent from the prior month. Building material and garden equipment and supplies dealers were essentially unchanged at $43.1 billion.

Food and beverage stores recorded sales of $81.4 billion, up 0.1 percent from October. Grocery stores, a subset of this category, had sales of $72.6 billion.

Clothing and clothing accessories stores had sales of $28.9 billion, down 0.2 percent from the previous month but up 2.3 percent from November 2023.

Gasoline stations reported sales of $55.7 billion, down 0.8 percent from October, largely reflecting lower gasoline prices.

Nonstore retailers, which includes e-commerce, reported sales of $119.8 billion, up 1.8 percent from October and up 10.2 percent from a year ago.

Food services and drinking places sales were $101.5 billion, an increase of 0.9 percent from October.

### Revisions

The August 2024 to September 2024 percent change was revised from down 0.3 percent to down 0.1 percent. The September 2024 to October 2024 percent change was revised from up 0.4 percent to up 0.5 percent.

### Notes

These estimates are based on a subsample of the Annual Retail Trade Survey and represent adjusted figures. The advance estimates are subject to revision based on more complete data. Seasonal adjustment factors are derived from the X-13ARIMA-SEATS program.

The Census Bureau notes that sampling error and other sources of uncertainty exist in these estimates. The coefficient of variation for total retail sales is approximately 0.5 percent.
