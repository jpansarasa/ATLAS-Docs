# Spin/Bias Extraction Test Cases

This synthetic document tests extraction accuracy across different editorial framings of the same data.

## Test Case 1: Unemployment Rate (4.1%)

### Neutral
The unemployment rate was 4.1 percent in December 2024.

### Positive Spin
The unemployment rate plunged to just 4.1 percent in December 2024, beating market expectations and signaling robust labor market health.

### Negative Spin
The unemployment rate remained stubbornly elevated at 4.1 percent in December 2024, disappointing analysts who had hoped for improvement.

### Hedged Language
The unemployment rate was approximately 4.1 percent in December 2024, though preliminary figures may be subject to revision.

### Negation (TRICKY)
The unemployment rate did not fall below 4.1 percent in December 2024.

### Double Negation (VERY TRICKY)
The unemployment rate was not above 4.1 percent, meaning it did not exceed this threshold in December 2024.

## Test Case 2: Job Gains with Comparison (150,000 vs 175,000 expected)

### Neutral
Nonfarm payrolls increased by 150,000 in December 2024. Economists had expected 175,000.

### Positive Spin
Despite forecasts calling for 175,000 jobs, the economy still managed to add a respectable 150,000 positions in December 2024.

### Negative Spin
The economy added a disappointing 150,000 jobs in December 2024, falling far short of the 175,000 expected by Wall Street analysts.

### Dismissive
A mere 150,000 jobs were added in December 2024, well below the 175,000 forecast.

## Test Case 3: GDP Growth with Range (2.0-2.5%)

### Neutral
GDP growth is expected to be between 2.0 and 2.5 percent in 2025.

### Positive Spin
GDP growth is expected to surge to as high as 2.5 percent in 2025, with even the lower bound of 2.0 percent representing solid expansion.

### Negative Spin
GDP growth may be as low as 2.0 percent in 2025, with even optimistic forecasts capping growth at just 2.5 percent.

## Test Case 4: Inflation with Direction

### Neutral
Inflation was 2.7 percent in November 2024, compared to 2.6 percent in October 2024.

### Positive Spin
Inflation remained well-contained at 2.7 percent in November 2024, barely budging from October's 2.6 percent and staying close to the Fed's target.

### Negative Spin
Inflation ticked up to 2.7 percent in November 2024, an unwelcome increase from October's 2.6 percent that could delay rate cuts.

## Test Case 5: Negation Edge Cases

### Simple Negation
The Fed did not raise rates in December 2024. The rate remained at 4.5 percent.

### Negative Change
Retail sales did not increase in November 2024, declining by 0.3 percent instead.

### Threshold Statement
Initial jobless claims did not exceed 220,000 for the week ending December 21, coming in at 219,000.

### Conditional Negation
If unemployment does not rise above 4.5 percent, the current 4.1 percent rate will likely persist.
