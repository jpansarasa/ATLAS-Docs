# Consumer Confidence Index - December 2024

## The Conference Board

**Release Date:** December 23, 2024
**Data Reference Period:** December 2024

The Conference Board Consumer Confidence Index decreased to 104.7 in December, down from 112.8 in November. The Present Situation Index—based on consumers' assessment of current business and labor market conditions—fell to 140.2 from 143.0. The Expectations Index—based on consumers' short-term outlook for income, business, and labor market conditions—declined to 81.1 from 92.3, falling below the threshold of 80 that often signals a recession.

"Consumer confidence dropped in December as views of current labor market conditions softened and consumers grew more pessimistic about future business conditions," said Dana M. Peterson, Chief Economist at The Conference Board.

### Present Situation

Consumers' assessment of current business conditions was less favorable in December:

- 20.4 percent of consumers said business conditions were "good," down from 21.5 percent.
- 17.3 percent said business conditions were "bad," up from 16.1 percent.

Consumers' assessment of the labor market was slightly less positive:

- 37.1 percent of consumers said jobs were "plentiful," down from 37.9 percent.
- 16.1 percent of consumers said jobs were "hard to get," up from 15.4 percent.

### Expectations

Consumers' outlook for the next six months became more pessimistic:

- 17.5 percent of consumers expect business conditions to improve, down from 20.2 percent.
- 14.2 percent expect business conditions to worsen, up from 10.5 percent.

Consumers' assessment of the labor market outlook was also less favorable:

- 18.2 percent of consumers expect more jobs to be available, down from 19.9 percent.
- 18.0 percent anticipate fewer jobs, up from 16.3 percent.

Income expectations declined slightly:

- 17.1 percent of consumers expect their incomes to increase, down from 18.4 percent.
- 12.3 percent expect their incomes to decrease, up from 11.8 percent.

### Buying Plans

Buying plans were mixed in December:

- 47.2 percent of consumers plan to buy a home in the next six months, down from 48.1 percent.
- 8.5 percent plan to buy an automobile, down from 9.2 percent.
- 52.3 percent plan major appliance purchases, up from 51.8 percent.
