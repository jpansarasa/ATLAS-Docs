# U.S. International Trade in Goods and Services - October 2024

## U.S. Census Bureau and U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis

**Release Date:** December 5, 2024
**Data Reference Period:** October 2024

The U.S. Census Bureau and the U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis announced today that the goods and services deficit was $73.8 billion in October, up $9.3 billion from $64.5 billion in September, revised.

October exports were $265.7 billion, $5.1 billion more than September exports. October imports were $339.6 billion, $14.4 billion more than September imports.

### Exports

The increase in exports in October reflected increases in:
- Capital goods ($3.4 billion) - primarily civilian aircraft
- Consumer goods ($1.0 billion)
- Industrial supplies and materials ($0.8 billion)

The largest export increases were to:
- Canada: +$1.8 billion
- Mexico: +$1.2 billion
- Japan: +$0.9 billion

Total goods exports were $175.8 billion in October, up $3.9 billion from September.
Total services exports were $89.9 billion in October, up $1.2 billion from September.

### Imports

The increase in imports in October reflected increases in:
- Capital goods ($6.2 billion) - primarily computers and semiconductors
- Consumer goods ($4.8 billion)
- Industrial supplies and materials ($2.1 billion)
- Automotive vehicles and parts ($1.5 billion)

The largest import increases were from:
- China: +$5.2 billion
- European Union: +$2.8 billion
- Mexico: +$2.1 billion

Total goods imports were $277.8 billion in October, up $12.3 billion from September.
Total services imports were $61.8 billion in October, up $2.1 billion from September.

### Trade Balances

The goods deficit increased $8.4 billion in October to $102.0 billion.
The services surplus increased $0.9 billion in October to $28.2 billion.

### Year-to-Date

For the first ten months of 2024:
- Exports totaled $2,591.4 billion, up 3.2 percent from the same period in 2023
- Imports totaled $3,238.7 billion, up 5.1 percent
- The goods and services deficit was $647.3 billion, up 18.3 percent

### Major Trading Partners

**Deficit with China:** $28.1 billion in October, up from $24.3 billion in September

**Deficit with European Union:** $21.5 billion in October, up from $19.2 billion in September

**Deficit with Mexico:** $15.8 billion in October, up from $13.9 billion in September

**Surplus with South and Central America:** $4.2 billion in October
