# ISM Manufacturing Report On Business - December 2024

## Institute for Supply Management

**Release Date:** January 3, 2025
**Data Reference Period:** December 2024

The December Manufacturing PMI registered 49.3 percent, up 0.9 percentage point from November's reading of 48.4 percent. This indicates contraction in the overall economy for the ninth consecutive month after 30 months of expansion.

The New Orders Index registered 52.5 percent, up 2.1 percentage points from November's reading of 50.4 percent.

The Production Index registered 50.3 percent, a 4.3-percentage point increase from November's reading of 46.0 percent.

The Prices Index registered 52.5 percent, up 2.2 percentage points from November's reading of 50.3 percent.

The Backlog of Orders Index registered 45.9 percent, down 0.3 percentage point from the 46.2 percent recorded in November.

The Employment Index registered 45.3 percent, down 2.8 percentage points from November's figure of 48.1 percent.

The Supplier Deliveries Index registered 52.5 percent, a 3.0-percentage point increase from November's figure of 49.5 percent.

The Inventories Index registered 48.4 percent, up 2.5 percentage points from November's reading of 45.9 percent.

The New Export Orders Index registered 50.1 percent, up 2.0 percentage points from November's figure of 48.1 percent.

The Imports Index registered 49.7 percent, a 1.0-percentage point increase from November's reading of 48.7 percent.

### Comments from Survey Respondents

"Business remains slow, and we continue to see project delays." (Chemical Products)

"Sales are up, but profitability is challenged by increased material costs." (Transportation Equipment)

"Tariff concerns are causing customers to place larger orders ahead of potential price increases." (Computer & Electronic Products)
