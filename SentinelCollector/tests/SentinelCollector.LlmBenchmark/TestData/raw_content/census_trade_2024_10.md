# U.S. International Trade in Goods and Services - October 2024

## U.S. Census Bureau and U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis

The U.S. Census Bureau and the U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis announced today that the goods and services deficit was $73.8 billion in October, up $9.5 billion from $64.3 billion in September, revised.

October exports were $265.7 billion, $2.0 billion less than September exports. October imports were $339.5 billion, $7.5 billion more than September imports.

### Goods and Services

The October decrease in the goods and services deficit reflected a decrease in the goods deficit of $8.6 billion to $98.3 billion and an increase in the services surplus of $0.9 billion to $24.5 billion.

Exports of goods decreased $3.1 billion to $172.6 billion in October. Exports of services increased $1.1 billion to $93.1 billion in October.

Imports of goods increased $5.5 billion to $270.9 billion in October. Imports of services increased $2.0 billion to $68.6 billion in October.

### Three-Month Moving Averages

For the three months ending in October, the average goods and services deficit was $69.2 billion, up $3.4 billion from the three months ending in July.

### Goods by Selected Countries and Areas

The deficit with China was $28.3 billion in October, up from $26.1 billion in September. Exports to China were $13.1 billion; imports from China were $41.4 billion.

The deficit with the European Union was $21.5 billion in October, down from $22.8 billion in September. Exports to the European Union were $29.4 billion; imports from the European Union were $50.9 billion.

The deficit with Mexico was $15.7 billion in October, up from $13.2 billion in September. Exports to Mexico were $28.9 billion; imports from Mexico were $44.6 billion.

The surplus with Canada was $1.2 billion in October, compared with a deficit of $0.8 billion in September. Exports to Canada were $31.7 billion; imports from Canada were $30.5 billion.

### Petroleum Trade

The petroleum deficit was $4.6 billion in October, up from $3.9 billion in September. Petroleum exports were $18.2 billion in October; petroleum imports were $22.8 billion.

Nominal petroleum imports increased $1.1 billion, reflecting higher volumes. The average price per barrel of crude oil imports was $73.41 in October, down from $74.82 in September.

### Year-to-Date Totals

For the first ten months of 2024, the goods and services deficit increased $80.1 billion, or 13.2 percent, from the same period in 2023. Exports increased $81.9 billion or 3.2 percent. Imports increased $162.0 billion or 5.5 percent.

The goods deficit increased $91.3 billion from the same period in 2023. The services surplus increased $11.2 billion from the same period in 2023.
