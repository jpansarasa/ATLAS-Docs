# New Residential Construction - November 2024

## U.S. Census Bureau and U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

The following new residential construction statistics for November 2024 were jointly announced today by the U.S. Census Bureau and the Department of Housing and Urban Development.

### Building Permits

Privately-owned housing units authorized by building permits in November were at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 1,416,000. This is 6.1 percent below the revised October rate of 1,508,000 and is 0.2 percent below the November 2023 rate of 1,419,000.

Single-family authorizations in November were at a rate of 972,000; this is 2.4 percent below the revised October figure of 996,000. Authorizations of units in buildings with five units or more were at a rate of 393,000 in November.

### Housing Starts

Privately-owned housing starts in November were at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 1,289,000. This is 1.8 percent below the revised October estimate of 1,312,000 and is 14.6 percent below the November 2023 rate of 1,510,000.

Single-family housing starts in November were at a rate of 927,000; this is 6.4 percent below the revised October figure of 990,000. The November rate for units in buildings with five units or more was 346,000.

### Housing Completions

Privately-owned housing completions in November were at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 1,604,000. This is 9.2 percent above the revised October estimate of 1,469,000 and is 13.5 percent above the November 2023 rate of 1,413,000.

Single-family housing completions in November were at a rate of 1,010,000; this is 4.7 percent above the revised October rate of 965,000. The November rate for units in buildings with five units or more was 582,000.

### Regional Breakdowns - Housing Starts

In the Northeast, housing starts were at an annual rate of 92,000 units in November, down 8.0 percent from October. The Midwest recorded housing starts at a rate of 161,000 units, up 2.5 percent from the prior month. The South saw housing starts at a rate of 754,000 units, representing 58.5 percent of total national starts. The West posted housing starts at a rate of 282,000 units in November, down 4.4 percent from October.

### Regional Breakdowns - Building Permits

Building permits in the Northeast were at an annual rate of 134,000 units. The Midwest recorded building permits at a rate of 194,000 units. The South issued building permits at a rate of 788,000 units, accounting for 55.6 percent of total national permits. Building permits in the West were at a rate of 300,000 units.

### Notes

The seasonally adjusted annual rate (SAAR) is the monthly rate multiplied by 12 to provide an annualized figure that accounts for seasonal patterns in construction activity. All rates are expressed in thousands of units. Estimates in this report are based on data from surveys and from administrative records.
