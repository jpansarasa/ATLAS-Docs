# Employment Situation Summary - January 2025

## Bureau of Labor Statistics

Total nonfarm payroll employment rose by 256,000 in December, and the unemployment rate changed little at 4.1 percent, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reported today. Employment trended up in health care, government, and social assistance. Retail trade lost jobs.

### Household Survey Data

The unemployment rate, at 4.1 percent, changed little in December. The number of unemployed persons, at 6.9 million, was also essentially unchanged over the month. These measures are higher than a year earlier, when the jobless rate was 3.7 percent and the number of unemployed persons was 6.3 million.

Among the major worker groups, the unemployment rate for adult men was 3.8 percent in December, while the rate for adult women was 3.7 percent. The unemployment rate for teenagers was 12.2 percent, essentially unchanged from November. The unemployment rates for Whites (3.6 percent), Blacks (6.1 percent), and Hispanics (4.9 percent) showed little change over the month.

The labor force participation rate, at 62.5 percent, was unchanged in December. The employment-population ratio was 60.0 percent in December, essentially unchanged from November.

### Establishment Survey Data

Total nonfarm payroll employment increased by 256,000 in December, following an average monthly gain of 173,000 over the prior 3 months. In 2024, payroll employment increased by an average of 186,000 per month, compared with an average monthly gain of 251,000 in 2023.

Health care added 46,000 jobs in December, higher than the average monthly gain of 42,000 over the prior 12 months. Ambulatory health care services added 21,000 jobs, and hospitals added 12,000 jobs.

Government employment increased by 33,000 in December. Employment in government has increased by 47,000 per month on average over the prior 12 months.

Social assistance employment rose by 23,000 in December.

Retail trade employment declined by 21,000 in December, following a gain of 24,000 in November. Over the year, retail trade has lost 41,000 jobs.

In December, average hourly earnings for all employees on private nonfarm payrolls increased by 0.3 percent to $35.69. Over the past 12 months, average hourly earnings have increased by 3.9 percent.

The average workweek for all employees on private nonfarm payrolls was 34.3 hours in December.

This news release presents statistics from two monthly surveys. The household survey measures labor force status, including unemployment, by demographic characteristics. The establishment survey measures nonfarm employment, hours, and earnings by industry.
