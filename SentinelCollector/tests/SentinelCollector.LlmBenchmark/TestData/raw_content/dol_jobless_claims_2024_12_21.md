# Unemployment Insurance Weekly Claims Report

## U.S. Department of Labor - Employment and Training Administration

### News Release - December 26, 2024

In the week ending December 21, the advance figure for seasonally adjusted initial claims was 219,000, an increase of 6,000 from the previous week's revised level. The previous week's level was revised up by 1,000 from 212,000 to 213,000. The 4-week moving average was 226,500, a decrease of 3,250 from the previous week's revised average. The previous week's average was revised up by 250 from 229,500 to 229,750.

The advance seasonally adjusted insured unemployment rate was 1.2 percent for the week ending December 14, unchanged from the previous week's unrevised rate.

The advance number for seasonally adjusted insured unemployment during the week ending December 14 was 1,910,000, an increase of 46,000 from the previous week's revised level. The previous week's level was revised up by 2,000 from 1,862,000 to 1,864,000. The 4-week moving average was 1,881,500, an increase of 15,750 from the previous week's revised average.

### Initial Claims by State

The highest insured unemployment rates in the week ending December 7 were in New Jersey (2.5 percent), California (2.3 percent), Rhode Island (2.2 percent), Massachusetts (2.1 percent), and New York (2.0 percent).

The largest increases in initial claims for the week ending December 14 were in California (+5,842), New York (+4,126), Texas (+2,891), Ohio (+2,317), and Michigan (+1,954), while the largest decreases were in Georgia (-3,215), Florida (-2,847), Pennsylvania (-1,892), Illinois (-1,456), and North Carolina (-1,123).

### Unadjusted Data

The unadjusted advance number of initial claims totaled 312,654 during the week ending December 21, an increase of 38,721 from the previous week. The seasonal factors had expected a decrease of 8,254 claims. There were 266,344 initial claims in the comparable week of 2023.

The advance number of actual initial claims under state programs, unadjusted, totaled 305,218 in the week ending December 21, an increase of 38,127 from the prior week.

### Extended Benefits and Emergency Programs

During the week ending December 7, there were 1,672 initial claims filed for Pandemic Unemployment Assistance. The total number of people claiming benefits in all programs for the week ending November 30 was 2,094,567.

### Technical Notes

The advance number of initial claims is subject to revision in the following week. The seasonal adjustment methodology may result in variations between reported and actual figures during holiday periods.
