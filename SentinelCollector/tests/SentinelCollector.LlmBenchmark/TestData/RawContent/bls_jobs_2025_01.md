# Employment Situation Summary - January 2025

## Bureau of Labor Statistics - U.S. Department of Labor

**Release Date:** January 10, 2025
**Data Reference Period:** December 2024

Total nonfarm payroll employment rose by 256,000 in December, and the unemployment rate changed little at 4.1 percent, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reported today.

Employment trended up in health care, government, and social assistance. Retail trade lost jobs.

### Household Survey Data

The unemployment rate edged down to 4.1 percent in December; it has remained in a narrow range of 4.0 percent to 4.2 percent since May 2024. The number of unemployed persons, at 6.9 million, was essentially unchanged.

Among the major worker groups, the unemployment rates for adult men (3.8 percent), adult women (3.7 percent), teenagers (12.1 percent), Whites (3.6 percent), Blacks (6.1 percent), Asians (3.7 percent), and Hispanics (4.9 percent) showed little change over the month.

The labor force participation rate, at 62.5 percent, was unchanged in December. The employment-population ratio was unchanged at 60.0 percent.

### Establishment Survey Data

Total nonfarm payroll employment increased by 256,000 in December, with gains in health care, government, and social assistance.

Health care added 46,000 jobs in December. Ambulatory health care services added 24,000 jobs, and hospitals added 13,000 jobs.

Government employment increased by 33,000 in December, about in line with the average monthly gain of 37,000 over the prior 12 months.

Social assistance added 23,000 jobs in December.

Retail trade employment declined by 21,000 in December, following a gain of 32,000 in November.

Employment in other major industries, including mining and logging, construction, manufacturing, wholesale trade, transportation and warehousing, information, financial activities, professional and business services, and leisure and hospitality, showed little change over the month.

In December, average hourly earnings for all employees on private nonfarm payrolls increased by 0.3 percent to $35.69. Over the past 12 months, average hourly earnings have increased by 3.9 percent.

The average workweek for all employees on private nonfarm payrolls was 34.3 hours in December, unchanged from November.

### Revisions

The change in total nonfarm payroll employment for October was revised down by 7,000, from +36,000 to +29,000, and the change for November was revised down by 5,000, from +227,000 to +222,000. With these revisions, employment in October and November combined is 12,000 lower than previously reported.
