# Consumer Price Index - November 2024

## Bureau of Labor Statistics - U.S. Department of Labor

**Release Date:** December 11, 2024
**Data Reference Period:** November 2024

The Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) increased 0.3 percent in November on a seasonally adjusted basis, the same increase as in October, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reported today. Over the last 12 months, the all items index increased 2.7 percent before seasonal adjustment.

The index for shelter rose 0.3 percent in November and was the largest contributor to the monthly all items increase. The food index increased 0.4 percent over the month, with the food at home index rising 0.5 percent.

The energy index increased 0.2 percent in November, after falling 1.0 percent in October. The gasoline index rose 0.6 percent over the month.

The index for all items less food and energy rose 0.3 percent in November. Indexes that increased in November include shelter, used cars and trucks, airline fares, and medical care. The indexes for apparel and communication decreased over the month.

### 12-Month Changes

The all items index rose 2.7 percent for the 12 months ending November 2024, an increase from the 2.6 percent rise over the 12 months ending October 2024.

The all items less food and energy index rose 3.3 percent over the last 12 months. The energy index fell 3.2 percent over the year, while the food index increased 2.4 percent.

### Food

The food index increased 0.4 percent in November after rising 0.2 percent in October. The food at home index rose 0.5 percent, and the food away from home index increased 0.3 percent.

Among food at home categories, the index for meats, poultry, fish, and eggs increased 1.7 percent in November. The index for eggs jumped 8.2 percent over the month.

Over the last 12 months, the food at home index rose 1.6 percent, while food away from home increased 3.6 percent.

### Energy

The energy index rose 0.2 percent in November after declining 1.0 percent in October.

The gasoline index increased 0.6 percent in November. The indexes for natural gas and electricity rose 0.3 percent and 0.4 percent, respectively.

Over the last 12 months, the energy index fell 3.2 percent. The gasoline index decreased 8.1 percent, while electricity rose 3.1 percent.

### All Items Less Food and Energy

The index for all items less food and energy rose 0.3 percent in November, the same increase as in October.

The shelter index rose 0.3 percent in November, with rent of primary residence up 0.3 percent and owners' equivalent rent up 0.2 percent.

The index for used cars and trucks increased 2.0 percent in November, following a 2.7 percent increase in October.

New vehicles were unchanged in November. The medical care index rose 0.4 percent, with hospital services up 0.5 percent.
